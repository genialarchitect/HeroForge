## 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1** "Applicable Data Protection Law" means all laws and regulations applicable to the Processing of Personal Data, including but not limited to:
- The General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR")
- The UK General Data Protection Regulation ("UK GDPR")
- The California Consumer Privacy Act ("CCPA") and California Privacy Rights Act ("CPRA")
- Any other applicable national data protection laws

**1.2** "Controller" means the natural or legal person which determines the purposes and means of the Processing of Personal Data.

**1.3** "Data Subject" means an identified or identifiable natural person whose Personal Data is Processed.

**1.4** "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.

**1.5** "Personal Data Breach" means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data.

**1.6** "Processing" means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, erasure, or destruction.

**1.7** "Processor" means a natural or legal person which Processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

**1.8** "Services" means the HeroForge cybersecurity platform and related services provided under the Main Agreement.

**1.9** "Sub-processor" means any Processor engaged by HeroForge to Process Personal Data on behalf of the Customer.

**1.10** "Main Agreement" means the Terms of Service or other agreement governing Customer's use of the Services.

**1.11** "Standard Contractual Clauses" or "SCCs" means the standard contractual clauses for the transfer of personal data to third countries approved by the European Commission.

## 2. SCOPE AND PURPOSE

**2.1 Scope**

This DPA applies to all Processing of Personal Data by HeroForge on behalf of Customer in connection with the Services.

**2.2 Relationship to Main Agreement**

This DPA is incorporated into and forms part of the Main Agreement. In the event of any conflict between this DPA and the Main Agreement, this DPA shall prevail with respect to the Processing of Personal Data.

**2.3 Duration**

This DPA shall remain in effect for the duration of the Main Agreement and shall automatically terminate upon termination of the Main Agreement, subject to the data deletion provisions herein.

---

## 3. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

**3.1 Customer as Controller**

Customer is the Controller of Personal Data and determines the purposes and means of Processing. Customer is responsible for:
- Ensuring a lawful basis for Processing
- Providing required notices to Data Subjects
- Responding to Data Subject requests (with HeroForge's assistance)
- Ensuring compliance with Applicable Data Protection Law
- Providing lawful instructions to HeroForge

**3.2 HeroForge as Processor**

HeroForge is the Processor of Personal Data and Processes Personal Data only on behalf of Customer. HeroForge shall:
- Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Customer
- Ensure persons authorized to Process Personal Data are bound by confidentiality
- Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures
- Assist Customer in responding to Data Subject requests
- Assist Customer with data protection impact assessments
- Delete or return Personal Data upon termination
- Make available information necessary to demonstrate compliance

## 6. SECURITY MEASURES

**6.1 Technical and Organizational Measures**

HeroForge implements and maintains appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data, including:

**Access Controls:**
- Role-based access control (RBAC)
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Unique user accounts with strong password requirements
- Regular access reviews and revocation procedures

**Encryption:**
- TLS 1.3 for data in transit
- AES-256 encryption for data at rest
- Database encryption with SQLCipher
- Encrypted backups

**Network Security:**
- Firewalls and network segmentation
- Intrusion detection and prevention systems
- DDoS protection
- Regular vulnerability scanning

**Physical Security:**
- Data center physical access controls
- Environmental controls
- Redundant infrastructure

**Operational Security:**
- Security awareness training for personnel
- Background checks for personnel with data access
- Incident response procedures
- Business continuity and disaster recovery plans

**Monitoring and Logging:**
- Security event logging
- Audit trail maintenance
- Anomaly detection

**6.2 Security Assessment**

HeroForge shall:
- Regularly assess and test the effectiveness of security measures
- Conduct annual penetration testing by qualified third parties
- Maintain relevant security certifications (SOC 2, when obtained)

**6.3 Security Documentation**

Upon request, HeroForge shall provide:
- Summary of security measures
- Third-party audit reports (under NDA)
- Penetration test executive summaries
- Security certification documentation

## 9. PERSONAL DATA BREACH

**9.1 Notification**

In the event of a Personal Data Breach affecting Customer's Personal Data, HeroForge shall:
- Notify Customer without undue delay and in any event within 48 hours
- Provide notification to: [designated contact email]

**9.2 Breach Notification Content**

Notification shall include, to the extent known:
- Description of the nature of the breach
- Categories and approximate number of Data Subjects affected
- Categories and approximate number of Personal Data records affected
- Name and contact details of HeroForge's data protection contact
- Description of likely consequences
- Description of measures taken or proposed to address the breach

**9.3 Investigation and Mitigation**

HeroForge shall:
- Promptly investigate the breach
- Take reasonable steps to mitigate effects
- Preserve evidence for forensic analysis
- Cooperate with Customer's investigation

**9.4 Customer Notification Obligations**

Customer is responsible for:
- Determining whether notification to supervisory authorities is required
- Determining whether notification to Data Subjects is required
- Making such notifications

**9.5 Documentation**

HeroForge shall maintain records of all Personal Data Breaches, including:
- Facts surrounding the breach
- Effects of the breach
- Remedial action taken

## 10. DATA RETENTION AND DELETION

**10.1 Retention During Agreement**

During the term of the Main Agreement, HeroForge shall retain Personal Data as necessary to provide the Services and as instructed by Customer.

**10.2 Deletion Upon Termination**

Upon termination of the Main Agreement:
- HeroForge shall delete all Personal Data within 90 days
- Deletion shall include all copies in production systems
- Backup copies shall be deleted within 180 days as they rotate

**10.3 Return of Data**

Upon Customer's written request prior to termination:
- HeroForge shall provide Personal Data in a standard, machine-readable format
- Export shall be provided within 30 days of request
- Customer may use self-service export features

**10.4 Exceptions to Deletion**

HeroForge may retain Personal Data after termination if:
- Required by Applicable Data Protection Law
- Required for legal claims or disputes
- Required for audit purposes (limited retention)

In such cases, HeroForge shall:
- Notify Customer of the retention and legal basis
- Implement access restrictions
- Delete when no longer required

**10.5 Certification**

Upon request, HeroForge shall certify in writing that Personal Data has been deleted in accordance with this DPA.
